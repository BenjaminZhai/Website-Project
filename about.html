THE TWELVE GREEK OLYMPIAN GODS

Zeus

Zeus is a god from the ancient greek religion. He is the king of the gods, and he is the god of the sky and weather. His roman counterpart is named Jupiter. He is the youngest out of his siblings, who are Hades, Poseidon, Hestia, Hera, and Demeter. He is the son of the titans Cronus and Rhea. He is also the husband of Hera, and the father of many other gods and heros, some who are also part of the twelve. He weilds a thunderbolt for a weapon.

Poseidon

Posieden is a god from greek mythology. He is the god of the sea(or water in general), earthquakes, and horse. His roman name is Neptune. He is the middle child out of his brothers. He is the son of the Titans Cronus and Rhea. He is married to the Nereid Amphrite. All horses and most cyclops are his children. He weilds a large trident for his weapon.

Hera

Hera is a goddess from the ancient greek religion. She is the queen of the gods, and the goddess of marriage. In roman, she is called Juno. She is the youngest out of her sisters. She is the daughter of Cronus and Rhea. She is the wife and sister of Zeus. She has 8 children; Ares, Angelos, Charites, Eilethyia, Enyo, Eris, Hebe, and Hephaestus. Her weapon is a scepter capped with a pomegranate and a cuckoo.

Demeter

Demeter is a goddess mentioned in greek mythology. She is the goddess of agriculture. Her roman counterpart is named Ceres. She is the middle child in her sisters. Her parents are Cronus and Rhea. Officially, she is not married, but she had two children (Persephone and Iachhus) due to her brother, Zeus. Her weapon was a long golden sword.

Athena

Athena is an ancient greek goddess. She is the goddess of wisdom and war. Her roman counterpart is known as Minerva. She has many half siblings, out of whom she is said to be the favourite. She is the daughter of Zeus, and a nymph, Metis. She is not married and offically has no children. She did adopt a son, Erichthonius, once however. She is fully decked out in battle armour, with a spear, sheild, and helmet. Her sheild, or sometimes cloak, dons the head of Medusa.

Aphrodite

Aphrodite is a greek goddess. She is the goddess of love and beauty. She is called Venus by the romans. There are two different myths surrounding her birth. One is that she is the daughter of Zeus and the Titan Dione. This would mean that she is also the half sibling of many different gods. However, the other myth states that she srpung up out of the foam of Ouranos when he died and fell into the sea. This would technically make her the sibling of the Titans, which include Cronus, Rhea, and many others. This would also technically make her the oldest in the current pantheon. She is married to the god Hephaestus, but she doesn't really like him, and has affairs all the time with Ares. Her godly children include Harmonia, the twin gods of fear and panic Phobos and Deimos, and Eros, the god of love. She doesn't really have a weapon, however she supposedly wears a cestus to "increase her female attraction".

Ares

Ares is a god from greek mythology. He is the god of war. His roman name is Mars. He has many half-siblings, all who are children of Zeus. His parents are Zeus and Hera. He is sometimes portrayed as Aphrodite's lover, sometimes her legitimate husband. He is the father of the twin gods of fear and panic, Phobos and Deimos. His weapons are the spear and sheild.

Hephaestus

Hephaestus is a god from the ancient greek religion. He is the god of fire, craftsmen, and blacksmiths. His roman name is Vulcan. His siblings are all the children of Zeus. His parents are Zeus and Hera. He is offically the husband of Aphrodite, but due to him being extremely deformed, she doesn't like him and often cheats on him with Ares. He has many children with mortals and immortals, but he doesn't have any with Aphrodite. He mostly makes the weapons for other gods, however in combat he uses a hammer from his forge.

Apollo

Apollo is an ancient greek god. He is the god of the Sun, the Oracle, music and poetry, and archers. In roman he is also called Apollo. He has a twin, Artemis, and has many half-siblings. His parents are Zeus and the titan Leto. He never offically married, but he did have many lovers. Due to this, he has many children. His weapon is probably a golden bow with a quiver of silver arrows.

Artemis

Artemis is an ancient greek goddess. She is the goddess of the moon, wild animals, the hunt, and of chastisity and childbirth. Her roman counterpart is called Diana. She is the twin to Apollo and the half sibling of many different gods and humans. Her parents are Zeus and Leto. Due to swearing chastisity at a young age, she never married or had any children. Her weapons include hunting material, such as bow, arrows, and hunting knives.

Hermes

Hermes is a greek god. He is the god of messengers, thieves, and also a patron of music. He is called Mercury in Rome. His half siblings are all the children of Zeus. His parents are Zeus, and the Pleiad Maia. He is not officially married to anyone, but he did have lots of children with many different gods. His weapon was a staff(Caduceus), that had two snakes entwined around it.

*Disclaimer: It is widely debated over who the final member was. However, the majority seems to believe that Dionysus replaced Hestia on the pantheon.

Dionysus

Dionysus is a god from greek mythology. He is the god of parties, wine, and madness. He is also known in Rome as Bacchus. He is half sibling to all of Zeus' other offspring. His parents are Zeus, and the mortal princess, Semele. Out of jealousy, Hera, Zeus' wife, convinced the pregnant Semele to ask Zeus to show his true form. Zeus begrudgingly agreed, but since his true form was too powerful for a mortal, Semele vaporized. Zeus, however, saved his son, by sewing him into his leg, until he was ready to be born. He is married to the former princess of Crete, and now divine Ariadne, who was abandoned by her previous husband, Theseus. He had nine children with her: Priapus, Hymen, Thoas, Staphylus, Oenopion, Comus, Phthonus, the Graces, and Deianira. His weapon is a thyrsus, which is a fennel-stem sceptre that was sometimes wound with ivy and dripping with honey.

Hestia (honorary mention)

Hestia is a greek mythological goddess. She is the goddess of the hearth, and of family. She is called Vesta by the Romans. She is the oldest out of all her siblings, being the firstborn of Cronus and Rhea. Hestia is a virgin goddess, and therefore has no children. She doesn't have a weapon, but she carries around a bowl of fire, symbolizing her duty as the goddess of the hearth.

Hades (Left Out)

Hades is a ancient greek god. He is the god of the underworld and death. He was left out of the 12 olympians because he had to take care of the underworld, so he couldn't really leave his post. Also, since he is the god of death, the other gods weren't to fond of him. He is called Pluto by the Romans. He is the oldest out of his brothers, Zeus and Posiedon. He is the child of Cronus and Rhea. He is married to the goddess of the spring, Persephone. He has some children, though they are all obsucre. His weapon is called a bident, which is basically a trident but with two prongs.
